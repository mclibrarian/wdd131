About American Girl History

American Girl History is a project dedicated to collecting knowledge of American Girl dolls in one place. American Girl is a brand of 18-inch dolls that focuses on teaching history and creating memorable characters through its products. While it was originally called Pleasant Company after its founder, Pleasant Rowland, the company was purchased by Mattel in 2002 and is currently sold under the American Girl brand name. American Girl is known for its research and quality, especially regarding its historical line, which features dolls that represent fictional girls who lived in historical eras from 1764-1999. It also has a contemporary line called Girl of the Year, which has continued since 2001, a baby doll line called Bitty Baby, and a line of 14-inch dolls called Welliewishers.

American Girl History focuses on the historical line of dolls, originally called The American Girls Collection, as well as the history of the company itself. The website contains ways to filter the dolls by several different categories and contains pictures and further information about each of the dolls. It also has a timeline page that contains a timeline of the company. It also has a contact page for people seeking further information that isn't available on the main website. AG History is run by Brita, a high school senior who thinks American Girl dolls are cool and wants to share her small pool of knowledge with everyone.

Further information:

Here is a list of other websites that may be of interest and provide more resources and information.

  • Americangirl.com, American Girl's official website. Here you can buy dolls, clothing, and accessories.
  • The American Girl Wiki, one of the most comprehensive collections of American Girl knowledge on the internet. There are articles for nearly every product American Girl has released.
  • American Girl Doll News, a blog run by a long-time American Girl fan. The best place to get information about new and upcoming releases.
  • Dandridge House Dolls, a blog with extremely detailed doll rooms and restoration advice. This is an excellent resource for fixing damaged dolls.
